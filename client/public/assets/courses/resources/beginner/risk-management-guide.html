Risk Management Rules & Formulas

CRITICAL PRINCIPLE: Risk management is not about avoiding losses—it's about surviving losses so you can profit from your wins. Proper risk management is the difference between professional and amateur traders.

1. The Golden Rules of Risk Management

Core Principles

1 Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade

This ensures that even a series of 10 consecutive losses will only reduce your account by 10-20%, keeping you in the game.

2 Always use a stop loss on every trade

No exceptions. A stop loss is your insurance policy. Trading without one is like driving without brakes.

3 Risk-Reward ratio minimum 1:2

For every $1 you risk, aim to make at least $2. This means you only need a 33% win rate to be profitable.

4 Maximum total portfolio risk: 6-10%

Limit simultaneous open positions so your total exposure never exceeds 6-10% of your account.

5 Plan every trade before entering

Know your entry, stop loss, and take profit BEFORE clicking the buy/sell button.

2. Essential Formulas

Position Sizing Formula

Basic Position Size Formula:

Position Size = (Account Balance × Risk %) / (Entry Price - Stop Loss Price)
Example 1 - Stock Trading:
• Account Balance: $10,000
• Risk per trade: 2% = $200
• Entry Price: $50.00
• Stop Loss: $48.00
• Risk per share: $2.00

Position Size = $200 / $2.00 = 100 shares
Total Investment = 100 shares × $50 = $5,000
Risk Amount = 100 shares × $2 = $200 (2% of account)
Example 2 - Forex Trading (EUR/USD):
• Account Balance: $10,000
• Risk per trade: 1% = $100
• Entry Price: 1.1250
• Stop Loss: 1.1200
• Pip Risk: 50 pips
• Pip Value (standard lot): $10/pip

Position Size = $100 / ($10 × 50 pips) = 0.20 lots
Risk Amount = 50 pips × $10/pip × 0.20 = $100

Risk-Reward Ratio

R:R Ratio = (Take Profit - Entry) / (Entry - Stop Loss)
Example:
• Entry: $100
• Stop Loss: $95 (risk $5)
• Take Profit: $110 (reward $10)

R:R = $10 / $5 = 1:2 (risking $1 to make $2)

Required Win Rate Formula

Win Rate Required = 1 / (1 + Risk:Reward Ratio)
R:R Ratio Breakeven Win Rate Interpretation
1:1 50% Need to win half your trades to break even
1:2 33% Only need 1 out of 3 trades to win
1:3 25% Only need 1 out of 4 trades to win
1:4 20% Only need 1 out of 5 trades to win

3. The 2% Rule in Practice

Why 2%?

Scenario: 10 Consecutive Losses

Risking 2% per trade:
Starting: $10,000 → After 10 losses: $8,171 (18.3% drawdown)
You're still in the game with 81.7% of your capital.

Risking 10% per trade:
Starting: $10,000 → After 10 losses: $3,487 (65% drawdown)
You've lost most of your account. Very hard to recover.

4. Advanced Risk Management

Scaling Risk Based on Confidence

  • High Confidence Setup (A+ setup): Risk 2%
  • Good Setup (B setup): Risk 1.5%
  • Marginal Setup (C setup): Risk 1% or skip
  • Uncertain Setup: Don't trade

Adjusting for Volatility

High Volatility Period: Reduce position size by 25-50%
Low Volatility Period: Can use standard position sizing
After Big Win: Don't increase risk - maintain discipline
After Big Loss: Consider reducing risk temporarily

Correlation Risk

Don't take multiple positions in highly correlated assets:

  • EUR/USD and GBP/USD move together ~80% of the time
  • Gold and Silver are highly correlated
  • Tech stocks often move together

Rule: If positions are 70%+ correlated, treat them as one position for risk calculation.

5. Money Management Strategies

The Pyramid Approach

Adding to winning positions (pyramiding):

  • Initial Position: Full size (e.g., 2% risk)
  • First Add: 50% of initial (e.g., 1% risk) after moving stop to breakeven
  • Second Add: 25% of initial (e.g., 0.5% risk) after securing profit
Never Average Down: Adding to losing positions increases risk and can lead to catastrophic losses. Only add to winners, never to losers.

Daily/Weekly Loss Limits

Limit Type Recommended Action When Hit
Daily Loss Limit 3-5% of account Stop trading for the day immediately
Weekly Loss Limit 8-10% of account Stop trading for the week, review strategy
Monthly Drawdown 15-20% of account Take a break, reassess approach, consider reducing size

6. Risk Management Checklist

Before Every Trade, Answer These:

1. How much am I risking on this trade? (Should be ≤2%)
2. Where is my stop loss? (Must be defined BEFORE entry)
3. What is my take profit target? (R:R should be ≥1:2)
4. What is my total portfolio risk? (Should be ≤10%)
5. Can I afford to be wrong on this trade? (Answer must be YES)
6. Have I sized this position correctly? (Use formula)
7. Is my stop loss at a logical level? (Not arbitrary)
8. What will I do if the trade goes against me? (Have a plan)
Remember: The goal is not to win every trade. The goal is to win more on your winners than you lose on your losers, while staying in the game long enough to profit from your edge.

7. Common Risk Management Mistakes

  • Moving stop losses further away: This turns small losses into big losses
  • Risking too much on "sure things": No trade is ever guaranteed
  • Not adjusting position size: Same lot size regardless of stop distance
  • Removing stop losses: Hoping a losing trade will turn around
  • Revenge trading: Doubling position size after a loss to "make it back"
  • Ignoring correlation: Taking 5 trades on correlated pairs = massive risk
  • No maximum loss limits: Blowing up entire account in one bad day
