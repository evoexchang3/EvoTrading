Technical Analysis Quick Reference Guide

Purpose: This guide provides a comprehensive overview of essential technical analysis concepts, patterns, and indicators used in trading. Keep this as a quick reference while analyzing charts.

1. Candlestick Patterns

Single Candlestick Patterns

Doji

Reversal/Indecision

Description: Opening and closing prices are virtually equal, creating a cross or plus sign shape.

Interpretation: Market indecision; potential reversal when at trend extremes.

Confirmation: Look for high volume and next candle's direction.

Hammer

Bullish Reversal

Description: Small body at top, long lower wick (2-3x body length), minimal upper wick.

Interpretation: Buyers rejected lower prices and pushed price back up.

Best Context: Bottom of downtrend with volume confirmation.

Shooting Star

Bearish Reversal

Description: Small body at bottom, long upper wick (2-3x body length), minimal lower wick.

Interpretation: Sellers rejected higher prices and pushed price back down.

Best Context: Top of uptrend with volume confirmation.

Engulfing Candle

Bullish Bearish

Description: Current candle's body completely engulfs previous candle's body.

Bullish: Green candle engulfs red candle at support.

Bearish: Red candle engulfs green candle at resistance.

Multi-Candlestick Patterns

Pattern Signal Description Key Points
Morning Star Bullish Reversal 3 candles: large red, small-bodied, large green Forms at bottom of downtrend; confirms buyers taking control
Evening Star Bearish Reversal 3 candles: large green, small-bodied, large red Forms at top of uptrend; confirms sellers taking control
Three White Soldiers Strong Bullish 3 consecutive large green candles with higher closes Strong buying pressure; each opens within previous body
Three Black Crows Strong Bearish 3 consecutive large red candles with lower closes Strong selling pressure; each opens within previous body

2. Support and Resistance

Golden Rule: Support and resistance levels are zones, not exact prices. Always consider a range rather than a specific price point.

Identifying Support/Resistance

  • Horizontal Levels: Price levels where price has reversed multiple times (2+ touches)
  • Round Numbers: Psychological levels (e.g., 1.2000, 50.00, 100.00) often act as SR
  • Previous Highs/Lows: Swing highs become resistance; swing lows become support
  • Moving Averages: 50-day, 100-day, and 200-day MAs often act as dynamic SR
  • Fibonacci Levels: 38.2%, 50%, and 61.8% retracement levels

Role Reversal Principle

Key Concept: When price breaks through resistance, that level often becomes new support (and vice versa).

  • Broken resistance → New support (in uptrend)
  • Broken support → New resistance (in downtrend)
  • More tests of a level = stronger the level
  • Volume on breakout confirms strength

3. Trend Lines and Channels

Drawing Trend Lines

Trend Type How to Draw Validation
Uptrend Line Connect 2+ higher lows with ascending line Third touch confirms validity; more touches = stronger trend
Downtrend Line Connect 2+ lower highs with descending line Third touch confirms validity; more touches = stronger trend
Channel Draw parallel line to trend line connecting opposite extremes Price should bounce between channel boundaries

Trading Trend Lines

  • Bounce Strategy: Buy/sell when price touches trend line with confirmation
  • Breakout Strategy: Trade breakout with volume and wait for retest
  • False Breakout: Price briefly breaks then returns; wait for confirmation
  • Angle Matters: 45-degree angle = sustainable; steeper = unsustainable

4. Key Technical Indicators

Moving Averages (MA)

Type Period Purpose Trading Signals
SMA 20 Short-term Quick trend identification Price above = bullish bias; below = bearish bias
EMA 50 Medium-term Trend confirmation, dynamic SR Crossover with 20 EMA signals trend change
SMA 200 Long-term Major trend, institutional level Above = bull market; below = bear market

Relative Strength Index (RSI)

  • Range: 0 to 100
  • Overbought: Above 70 (potential reversal down)
  • Oversold: Below 30 (potential reversal up)
  • Divergence: Price makes new high but RSI doesn't = bearish divergence
  • Best Use: Confirm momentum; don't use alone for entries

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)

  • Components: MACD line, Signal line, Histogram
  • Bullish Signal: MACD crosses above signal line
  • Bearish Signal: MACD crosses below signal line
  • Zero Line: Crossing above = bullish momentum; below = bearish momentum
  • Divergence: Powerful reversal signal when price and MACD disagree

Bollinger Bands

  • Structure: Middle band (20 SMA), Upper/Lower bands (2 standard deviations)
  • Squeeze: Bands narrow = low volatility, breakout imminent
  • Expansion: Bands widen = high volatility, strong trend
  • Mean Reversion: Price at upper band may reverse to middle band
  • Band Walk: In strong trends, price can ride the upper/lower band

5. Volume Analysis

Volume Principle: Volume confirms price movement. High volume = strong conviction; low volume = weak move.

Volume Interpretation

Scenario Interpretation Action
Price up + Volume up Strong bullish conviction Confirm uptrend continuation
Price up + Volume down Weak buying, potential reversal Be cautious, look for reversal signs
Price down + Volume up Strong bearish conviction Confirm downtrend continuation
Price down + Volume down Weak selling, potential reversal Watch for bullish reversal patterns
Breakout + High volume Valid breakout Consider entry with confirmation
Breakout + Low volume False breakout likely Wait for retest or avoid

6. Quick Reference Checklist

Before Every Trade:
  • Identify the overall trend (uptrend, downtrend, or range)
  • Mark key support and resistance levels on your chart
  • Check for confluence (multiple signals aligning)
  • Confirm with volume analysis
  • Use at least 2-3 indicators to confirm your bias
  • Never trade based on a single indicator or pattern

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Trading against the major trend
  • Ignoring volume confirmation
  • Drawing trend lines to fit your bias
  • Using too many indicators (analysis paralysis)
  • Entering without clear invalidation point
  • Forgetting that indicators lag price action
