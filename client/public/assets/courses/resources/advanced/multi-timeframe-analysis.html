Multi-Timeframe Analysis Framework

CORE PRINCIPLE: Trading a single timeframe is like driving while looking only at the road 5 feet in front of you. Multi-timeframe analysis reveals the full market picture - where we are, where we're going, and how to get there safely.

1. The Multi-Timeframe Hierarchy

Foundation Concept: Every timeframe tells a story, but they must align. Higher timeframes show the big picture (trend), while lower timeframes show precision entry opportunities. Your job is to trade in the direction of higher timeframes using signals from lower timeframes.

Standard Timeframe Structure

Long-Term Timeframe (HTF - Higher Timeframe)

Purpose: Identify overall trend direction and major support/resistance
For Day Trading: Daily, 4-Hour
For Swing Trading: Weekly, Daily
For Position Trading: Monthly, Weekly

What to Look For:
  • Overall trend direction (up, down, or sideways)
  • Major support and resistance zones
  • Key moving averages (200 MA, 50 MA)
  • Long-term chart patterns
  • Market structure (higher highs/lows or lower highs/lows)

Medium-Term Timeframe (MTF - Medium Timeframe)

Purpose: Identify intermediate trends and swing points
For Day Trading: 1-Hour, 15-Minute
For Swing Trading: 4-Hour, 1-Hour
For Position Trading: Daily, 4-Hour

What to Look For:
  • Pullbacks within HTF trend
  • Swing highs and swing lows
  • Continuation patterns (flags, pennants)
  • Reversal signals that align with HTF
  • Dynamic support/resistance (20/50 EMA)

Short-Term Timeframe (LTF - Lower Timeframe)

Purpose: Precise entry timing and trade management
For Day Trading: 5-Minute, 1-Minute
For Swing Trading: 15-Minute, 5-Minute
For Position Trading: 1-Hour, 15-Minute

What to Look For:
  • Specific entry signals (candlestick patterns)
  • Exact entry and stop loss placement
  • Momentum confirmation (RSI, MACD)
  • Volume spikes and confirmations
  • Intraday support/resistance for entries

2. The MTF Analysis Process

Phase 1 Higher Timeframe Analysis

Step 1: Determine the Trend
  • Uptrend: Series of higher highs and higher lows
  • Downtrend: Series of lower highs and lower lows
  • Range/Sideways: Horizontal price action between support/resistance
Step 2: Mark Key Levels
  • Draw horizontal support and resistance from swing points
  • Identify major round numbers (psychological levels)
  • Mark previous breakout points (become new SR)
  • Note 200 MA and 50 MA positions
Step 3: Assess Market Phase
  • Strong trend (moving away from consolidation)
  • Consolidation (building energy for next move)
  • Exhaustion (trend nearing completion)
  • Reversal (trend changing direction)
Example - HTF Analysis (Daily Chart):
EUR/USD Daily: Clear uptrend with higher highs and higher lows. Price is above 200 MA. Strong support at 1.0800. Resistance at 1.1000. Trend is strong, not exhausted. Bias: Look for buys only.

Phase 2 Medium Timeframe Confirmation

Step 1: Identify Pullbacks
  • Look for retracements within HTF trend
  • Fibonacci levels (38.2%, 50%, 61.8% retracements)
  • Pullbacks to moving averages (20 EMA, 50 EMA)
  • Minor support/resistance zones
Step 2: Recognize Patterns
  • Continuation patterns (flags, pennants) in HTF direction
  • Corrective patterns (ABC, triangles)
  • Avoid counter-trend patterns unless very strong
Step 3: Wait for Alignment
  • MTF trend must align with HTF trend
  • If MTF shows counter-trend, wait for HTF confirmation
  • Best entries occur when MTF reverses back into HTF direction
Example - MTF Analysis (4-Hour Chart):
EUR/USD pulls back from 1.0950 to 1.0850 (within Daily uptrend). Finds support at 50 EMA. Bullish engulfing candle forms. Volume increasing. Setup: Potential buy signal forming.

Phase 3 Lower Timeframe Entry

Step 1: Wait for Trigger
  • Reversal candlestick patterns (hammer, engulfing, morning star)
  • Breakout from consolidation
  • Moving average crossover (5 EMA crosses 20 EMA)
  • Momentum confirmation (RSI crosses above 50, MACD crossover)
Step 2: Confirm with Volume
  • Volume should spike on entry signal
  • Above-average volume validates the move
  • Low volume = wait for better signal
Step 3: Execute with Precision
  • Enter on close of signal candle or pullback
  • Stop loss below recent swing low (or above swing high for shorts)
  • Target based on HTF objectives (next resistance level)
Example - LTF Entry (15-Minute Chart):
EUR/USD bounces from 1.0850 with bullish engulfing on 4H. On 15-min chart, price breaks above 1.0860 mini-resistance with volume spike. Entry: 1.0862, Stop: 1.0840, Target: 1.0950 (R:R = 1:4)

3. MTF Trade Scenarios

Scenario A: Full Alignment (Ideal Setup)

Timeframe Status Signal
HTF (Daily) Strong uptrend Price above 200 MA, higher highs/lows
MTF (4-Hour) Pullback complete Bounced from 50 EMA, bullish pattern forming
LTF (15-Min) Entry trigger Break of mini-resistance with volume

Action: TAKE THE TRADE - All timeframes aligned. Highest probability setup.

Scenario B: Partial Alignment (Caution)

Timeframe Status Signal
HTF (Daily) Uptrend Price above 200 MA but approaching resistance
MTF (4-Hour) Sideways Consolidating, no clear direction
LTF (15-Min) Buy signal Bullish pattern on LTF

Action: REDUCE SIZE OR SKIP - Not all timeframes confirm. If taking trade, use 50% normal size.

Scenario C: Conflict (Avoid)

Timeframe Status Signal
HTF (Daily) Downtrend Price below 200 MA, lower highs/lows
MTF (4-Hour) Short-term bounce Counter-trend rally
LTF (15-Min) Buy signal Bullish candle pattern

Action: DO NOT TRADE - Buying into a downtrend. Wait for HTF to turn or look for sells instead.

4. Common MTF Strategies

Strategy #1: Trend + Pullback

  • HTF: Identify strong trend (up or down)
  • MTF: Wait for pullback to key support (uptrend) or resistance (downtrend)
  • LTF: Enter on reversal signal in trend direction
Best For: Swing trading, position trading
Win Rate: 65-70% when properly executed

Strategy #2: Breakout Confirmation

  • HTF: Identify consolidation or range
  • MTF: Watch for breakout with volume
  • LTF: Enter on pullback to broken level (retest)
Best For: All trading styles
Win Rate: 55-65% (higher when HTF shows direction)

Strategy #3: Triple Screen Trading System

  • Screen 1 (HTF): Determine trend with weekly chart
  • Screen 2 (MTF): Use daily oscillator (RSI/Stochastic) for timing
  • Screen 3 (LTF): Use hourly breakout for entry
Best For: Position trading
Win Rate: 60-75% with strict discipline

5. MTF Risk Management

Stop Loss Placement

Based On Stop Placement Pros Cons
HTF Level Below HTF support (buy) / Above HTF resistance (sell) Wider, less likely to get stopped out Larger position risk
MTF Level Below MTF swing low/high Balanced risk/reward Moderate stop distance
LTF Level Below LTF pattern low/high Tight stop, small risk Higher chance of stop hit on noise
Recommendation: Use MTF-based stops for most trades. If HTF is very strong, can use HTF stops with reduced position size. Avoid LTF-only stops unless scalping.

Target Setting

  • Conservative: Previous MTF swing high/low
  • Moderate: HTF support/resistance level
  • Aggressive: HTF measured move target

6. MTF Checklist

Before Every Trade:

Higher Timeframe (HTF):
□ Trend identified clearly (up/down/sideways)
□ Major SR levels marked on chart
□ Know where price is in relation to 200 MA
□ Understand market phase (trending, consolidating, reversing)

Medium Timeframe (MTF):
□ Current movement aligns with HTF trend
□ Pullback/consolidation is complete or completing
□ Pattern forming in direction of HTF
□ Key support/resistance identified

Lower Timeframe (LTF):
□ Clear entry trigger present
□ Volume confirms the move
□ Can place logical stop loss
□ R:R ratio is favorable (minimum 1:2)

Alignment Check:
□ All three timeframes tell the same story
□ No conflicting signals between timeframes
□ Trade direction matches HTF bias

If 100% aligned = Full position size
If 70-90% aligned = Reduced size (50%)
If <70% aligned = Skip the trade

7. Common MTF Mistakes

  • Mistake: Trading LTF signals that contradict HTF trend
    Fix: Always know HTF bias before looking at entries
  • Mistake: Using same indicators on all timeframes
    Fix: HTF = trend tools (MA), MTF = swing tools (SR), LTF = momentum (RSI, MACD)
  • Mistake: Not waiting for all timeframes to align
    Fix: Be patient - best setups have full alignment
  • Mistake: Setting stops based only on LTF
    Fix: Use MTF or HTF levels for stops to avoid noise
  • Mistake: Ignoring HTF context when scalping
    Fix: Even 1-minute scalps should respect Daily trend
