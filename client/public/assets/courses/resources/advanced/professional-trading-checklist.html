Professional Trading Checklist

Purpose: This comprehensive checklist ensures systematic trade evaluation and execution. Professional traders follow a process, not emotions. Use this before, during, and after every trade to maintain discipline and consistency.

Pre-Market Preparation (Daily)

Morning Routine

Mental State Check
□ Am I well-rested and alert?
□ Am I emotionally balanced (not angry, frustrated, or overconfident)?
□ Have I reviewed yesterday's trades and journal entries?
□ Do I have a clear head for objective decision-making?
If any answer is NO, consider not trading today. Your capital is precious.
Market Analysis
□ Reviewed major market news and economic calendar
□ Identified high-impact news events for the day
□ Checked overall market sentiment (risk-on vs risk-off)
□ Analyzed major indices and correlations
□ Noted key levels on watchlist instruments
Trading Plan Review
□ Reviewed my trading strategy and rules
□ Set maximum number of trades for today (e.g., 3-5)
□ Set maximum loss limit for the day (e.g., 3-5%)
□ Identified highest-probability setups for today
□ Prepared watchlist with potential trades

Pre-Trade Analysis

Setup Identification & Validation

Multi-Timeframe Analysis
□ HTF (Higher Timeframe): Trend direction identified
□ HTF: Major support/resistance levels marked
□ MTF (Medium Timeframe): Pullback/pattern identified
□ MTF: Setup aligns with HTF trend
□ LTF (Lower Timeframe): Entry trigger confirmed
□ ALL timeframes tell the same story
Trade Criteria Checklist
□ This setup matches my written strategy (not improvising)
□ Entry signal is clear and objective (not subjective)
□ Trade direction aligns with HTF trend
□ Setup has 3+ confirmation factors (e.g., SR level + pattern + volume)
□ This is an A+ setup (my highest confidence category)
If <80% of boxes checked: SKIP THIS TRADE. Wait for better setup.
Risk Assessment
□ Stop loss level is logical (not arbitrary)
□ Stop loss distance is reasonable (not too wide or too tight)
□ Risk per trade is 1-2% of account (maximum)
□ R:R ratio is minimum 1:2 (preferably 1:3 or better)
□ Total portfolio risk is under 6-10% limit
□ I can afford to lose this trade without emotional impact
Technical Confirmation
□ Volume pattern supports the setup
□ No major news events coming in next 2 hours
□ Market structure supports this direction
□ Key levels are clearly defined
□ No divergence between price and indicators (or divergence supports trade)

Trade Execution

Entry Preparation

Before Clicking Buy/Sell
□ Entry price is clearly defined
□ Stop loss price is set (not mental stop)
□ Take profit target(s) identified
□ Position size calculated correctly
□ Confirmed I have buying power/margin available
□ One last check: Am I entering out of FOMO or discipline?
Order Placement
□ Order type selected correctly (market, limit, stop)
□ Position size entered correctly (double-checked)
□ Stop loss order placed immediately after entry
□ Take profit order placed (or level marked)
□ Screenshot of entry taken for journal
□ Trade logged with entry time, price, reasoning

During Trade Management

Active Trade Monitoring

Position Management
□ Monitoring trade without obsessing (avoid constant checking)
□ Trailing stop in place if appropriate
□ Not moving stop loss away from entry (only tighter)
□ Respecting my take profit levels
□ Not adding to losers (averaging down)
□ Only adding to winners with proper risk management
Exit Criteria Evaluation
□ Am I being stopped out? (Accept it with discipline)
□ Is target reached? (Take profit as planned)
□ Has market structure changed? (Re-evaluate)
□ Did unexpected news occur? (Assess impact)
□ Is trade invalidated by new information? (Exit if yes)
Emotional Management
□ Not feeling urge to move stop loss
□ Not panicking on normal pullbacks
□ Not getting greedy if in profit
□ Following my plan, not my emotions
□ If feeling emotional: step away from charts

Post-Trade Review

Trade Documentation (Mandatory)

Journal Entry
□ Entry price, exit price, and time logged
□ Screenshots of entry and exit attached
□ Reasoning for entry written down
□ Emotions during trade documented
□ Profit/loss amount recorded
□ R:R ratio calculated for this trade
Trade Analysis
□ Did I follow my trading plan? (Yes/No - if No, why?)
□ What did I do well?
□ What could I improve?
□ Was this trade avoidable (if loss)?
□ What did I learn from this trade?
□ Would I take this exact trade again?
Performance Tracking
□ Updated trading statistics (win rate, profit factor)
□ Updated P&L for the day/week/month
□ Checked if approaching daily/weekly loss limits
□ Reviewed equity curve trajectory
□ Identified any patterns in winners/losers

End of Day Review

Daily Wrap-Up

Performance Review
□ All trades logged and analyzed
□ Daily P&L calculated
□ Win rate for the day calculated
□ Best trade of the day identified (and why)
□ Worst trade of the day identified (and why)
□ Noted any patterns or lessons learned
Plan for Tomorrow
□ Updated watchlist for tomorrow
□ Marked key levels for tomorrow's trading
□ Noted upcoming economic events
□ Identified potential setups to watch
□ Set alerts for key price levels
Self-Assessment
□ Did I follow my trading plan today?
□ Did I maintain discipline throughout?
□ Did I respect my loss limits?
□ Am I satisfied with my process (regardless of P&L)?
□ What one thing will I improve tomorrow?

Weekly Review

Review Area Questions to Answer
Performance □ Weekly P&L vs target
□ Win rate for the week
□ Average R:R ratio
□ Largest winner and loser
□ Number of trades vs plan
Execution □ % of trades that followed plan
□ Most common rule breaks
□ Trade quality (A+, B, C setups)
□ Emotional management rating
□ Best and worst trading day
Strategy □ Which setups worked best?
□ Which setups failed most?
□ Any patterns in winners?
□ Any patterns in losers?
□ Strategy adjustments needed?
Development □ Key lessons learned this week
□ Mistakes to avoid next week
□ Skills to practice this week
□ Goals for next week
□ Resources to study

Red Flags & Stop Trading Triggers

STOP TRADING IMMEDIATELY if:

Emotional Red Flags:
■ Feeling angry or revenge-trading after a loss
■ Overconfident after big win, taking excessive risks
■ Trading out of boredom, not strategy
■ Deviating from plan repeatedly
■ Making impulsive decisions

Performance Red Flags:
■ Hit daily loss limit (3-5% of account)
■ Three consecutive losing trades
■ Win rate drops below 40% for the week
■ Average loss exceeds average win
■ Breaking trading rules repeatedly

Action: Close all positions, step away from charts, clear your head. Don't trade for rest of day/week depending on severity.

Monthly Performance Review

Comprehensive Monthly Analysis:

Statistics to Calculate:
• Total trades taken
• Win rate percentage
• Profit factor (gross profit / gross loss)
• Average R:R ratio
• Expectancy [(Win% × Avg Win) - (Loss% × Avg Loss)]
• Maximum drawdown
• Return on account (monthly %)
• Sharpe ratio (if applicable)

Questions to Answer:
1. Did I meet my monthly target?
2. Was my process consistent?
3. Which strategies performed best/worst?
4. What were my most common mistakes?
5. What will I do differently next month?
6. Do I need to adjust my strategy?
7. Am I improving over time?
8. What skills do I need to develop?

Professional Habits

Daily Habits of Successful Traders:

1. Journaling: Document every trade without exception
2. Consistency: Trade the same strategy every day
3. Discipline: Follow rules even when it's hard
4. Learning: Review 1 losing trade per day in detail
5. Patience: Wait for A+ setups, skip marginal ones
6. Objectivity: Treat wins and losses the same way
7. Risk Management: Never compromise on position sizing
8. Adaptation: Update strategy when market changes
9. Health: Prioritize sleep, exercise, mental health
10. Continuous Improvement: Always seek to learn and grow

Final Reminder

Success in trading comes from:

• 10% finding good setups
• 10% executing trades correctly
• 80% following this checklist with discipline

The difference between profitable and losing traders is not intelligence or secret strategies—it's consistent execution of a systematic process. This checklist is your process. Follow it religiously, and success will follow.
