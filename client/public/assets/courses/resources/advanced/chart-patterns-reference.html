Advanced Chart Patterns Reference

Pattern Trading Essentials: Chart patterns are formations created by price movement that tend to repeat due to market psychology. Successful pattern trading requires: (1) Pattern identification, (2) Volume confirmation, (3) Proper entry timing, and (4) Risk management.

1. Reversal Patterns

Head and Shoulders (Top)

Bearish Reversal

Structure:

  • Left Shoulder: Rally to peak, pullback on decreasing volume
  • Head: Rally to higher peak (new high), pullback to neckline
  • Right Shoulder: Rally to lower peak (similar height to left shoulder), breaks neckline
  • Neckline: Support line connecting the two troughs
Trading Stats:
• Success Rate: ~83%
• Average Decline: 14-21% from neckline break
• Target: Distance from head to neckline, projected downward from break

Entry Strategy:

  • Conservative: Enter on neckline break with close below neckline on high volume
  • Aggressive: Enter on right shoulder formation completion
  • Confirmation: Wait for pullback to broken neckline (now resistance)

Volume Pattern: Left shoulder (high) → Head (lower) → Right shoulder (lowest) → Break (spike)

Stop Loss: Above right shoulder or above broken neckline on retest

Common Mistake: Trading before neckline break. Many "head and shoulders" patterns fail. Always wait for confirmation.

Inverse Head and Shoulders (Bottom)

Bullish Reversal

Structure: Mirror image of H&S top - forms at bottoms of downtrends

  • Left shoulder: Decline to trough, rally on decreasing volume
  • Head: Decline to lower trough, rally to neckline
  • Right shoulder: Decline to higher trough, breaks above neckline
Trading Stats:
• Success Rate: ~85%
• Average Advance: 19-38% from neckline break
• Target: Distance from head to neckline, projected upward

Key Requirements:

  • Clear prior downtrend (can't reverse if no trend exists)
  • Volume increasing through formation (dry up on head)
  • Strong volume spike on neckline break (essential)
  • Right shoulder MUST be higher than head

Stop Loss: Below right shoulder or below broken neckline on retest

Double Top

Bearish Reversal

Identification:

  • Two peaks at approximately the same price level
  • Trough between peaks (support level)
  • Second peak fails to exceed first peak
  • Breakdown occurs when price breaks support/trough
Trading Stats:
• Success Rate: ~74%
• Average Decline: 8-16%
• Target: Distance from peaks to trough, measured down from break

Time Between Peaks: Typically 1-3 months (short-term) or 3-12 months (long-term)

Volume Pattern:

  • First peak: Higher volume
  • Trough: Volume decreases
  • Second peak: Lower volume (key sign of weakness)
  • Breakdown: Volume spike confirms

Trading Strategy:

  • Enter short on break below trough with volume confirmation
  • Wait for retest of broken support (now resistance)
  • Stop above second peak or above broken support
  • Target = (Peak - Trough) distance projected from break

Double Bottom

Bullish Reversal

Identification:

  • Two troughs at approximately the same price level
  • Peak between troughs (resistance level)
  • Second trough fails to break below first trough
  • Breakout when price exceeds peak/resistance
Trading Stats:
• Success Rate: ~78%
• Average Advance: 12-24%
• Perfect validation: Second bottom slightly higher than first (higher low)

Entry Methods:

  • Aggressive: Enter on bounce from second bottom
  • Conservative: Wait for breakout above peak resistance
  • Best: Enter on pullback after breakout (retest)

Stop Loss: Below second bottom or below broken resistance on retest

2. Continuation Patterns

Ascending Triangle

Bullish Continuation

Structure:

  • Flat upper resistance (horizontal line at approximately same level)
  • Rising lower support (ascending trendline connecting higher lows)
  • Forms during uptrends as brief consolidation
  • Price "coils" between converging lines
Trading Stats:
• Success Rate: ~72% (upward breakout)
• Average Gain: 17-35%
• Breakout Direction: 81% break upward

Psychology: Buyers becoming more aggressive (higher lows) while sellers defend resistance. Eventually buyers overwhelm sellers.

Trading Rules:

  • Minimum 2 touches on resistance, 2 higher lows on support
  • Volume should decrease as pattern develops
  • Volume spike required on breakout (validates move)
  • Enter on break above resistance with volume
  • Target: Height of triangle added to breakout point
  • Stop: Below last higher low or below triangle support
Pro Tip: Ascending triangles that form after strong uptrends and break up have highest success rates. Avoid if formed after long consolidation periods.

Descending Triangle

Bearish Continuation

Structure:

  • Flat lower support (horizontal line at approximately same level)
  • Descending upper resistance (descending trendline connecting lower highs)
  • Forms during downtrends as consolidation
Trading Stats:
• Success Rate: ~68% (downward breakout)
• Average Decline: 12-21%
• Breakout Direction: 64% break downward

Psychology: Sellers becoming more aggressive (lower highs) while buyers defend support. Support eventually gives way.

Trading Strategy:

  • Enter short on break below support with volume
  • Target: Triangle height subtracted from breakdown point
  • Stop: Above last lower high or above triangle resistance
  • Watch for false breakdowns - wait for retest

Symmetrical Triangle

Neutral Continuation

Structure:

  • Converging trendlines: ascending support + descending resistance
  • Creates a coiling, spring-like formation
  • Breakout can be in either direction
  • Typically continues in direction of prior trend
Trading Stats:
• Success Rate: ~75% (in trend direction)
• Breakout typically occurs 2/3 through pattern
• Earlier breakout = stronger move

Trading Approach:

  • Identify prior trend - expect continuation in that direction
  • Volume decreases as triangle forms (coiling)
  • Strong volume spike on breakout is ESSENTIAL
  • Enter on breakout in direction of prior trend
  • Low volume breakout = likely false signal

Stop Loss Placement:

  • Bullish break: Stop below triangle support
  • Bearish break: Stop above triangle resistance
  • Conservative: Stop just inside triangle on opposite side

Flags and Pennants

Strong Continuation

Flag Pattern:

  • Strong, sharp move (flagpole)
  • Rectangular consolidation against trend (flag)
  • Bullish flag: slopes slightly down
  • Bearish flag: slopes slightly up
  • Breakout continues in flagpole direction

Pennant Pattern:

  • Sharp move (flagpole)
  • Small symmetrical triangle (pennant)
  • Very short formation (1-3 weeks)
  • Breakout in flagpole direction
Trading Stats:
• Success Rate: 68-78%
• Target: Length of flagpole = expected move from breakout
• Duration: Flags (1-8 weeks), Pennants (1-3 weeks)

Key Characteristics:

  • Must have sharp, nearly vertical flagpole
  • Volume high on flagpole, low during consolidation
  • Volume spike on breakout confirms pattern
  • Enter on breakout from consolidation
  • Stop below flag (bullish) or above flag (bearish)

3. Pattern Trading Guidelines

Volume Requirements

Pattern Phase Expected Volume Significance
Pattern Formation Decreasing Shows consolidation, indecision
Breakout Strong Spike (2-3x average) Validates the move, shows conviction
Pullback/Retest Lower than breakout Healthy consolidation, not rejection
Continuation Above average Confirms trend continuation

Pattern Reliability Factors

Higher Success When:
  • Pattern forms in direction of major trend
  • Volume behavior matches expected profile
  • Pattern is well-formed and symmetrical
  • Breakout occurs with strong volume spike
  • Pattern forms over appropriate timeframe (not too quick)
  • Prior trend was strong before pattern formation
Lower Success When:
  • Pattern goes against major trend
  • Volume doesn't spike on breakout
  • Pattern is asymmetrical or poorly formed
  • Forms after extended consolidation (no prior trend)
  • Too many false breakouts during formation

Common Pattern Failures

Failure Type How to Recognize Action
False Breakout Price breaks level but quickly reverses, low volume Exit immediately, wait for real breakout
Weak Breakout Breakout on low volume, no continuation Tighten stop, exit if pullback exceeds 50% of move
Pattern Negation Price breaks opposite direction with volume Exit pattern trade, consider reverse trade
Extended Pattern Pattern takes too long to complete (2x+ normal time) Lose faith in pattern, look for alternative setups

4. Pattern Trading Checklist

Before Trading Any Pattern:

1. Pattern Identification
□ Pattern is clearly identifiable and well-formed
□ Meets minimum criteria (touches, symmetry, timeframe)
□ Not forcing pattern to fit - it's obvious

2. Trend Context
□ Identified overall market trend
□ Pattern aligns with or against trend (know which)
□ Pattern fits context (reversal at extremes, continuation in trend)

3. Volume Confirmation
□ Volume decreased during pattern formation
□ Volume spiked on breakout (2-3x average minimum)
□ Volume profile matches pattern expectations

4. Entry Setup
□ Clear breakout level identified
□ Waiting for clean break + close beyond level
□ Entry price, stop loss, and take profit all planned
□ Position sized based on stop distance

5. Risk Management
□ Stop loss at logical level (not arbitrary)
□ Risk is 1-2% of account maximum
□ R:R ratio is minimum 1:2
□ Know what invalidates pattern (exit plan)

If ANY box is unchecked, don't trade the pattern.
